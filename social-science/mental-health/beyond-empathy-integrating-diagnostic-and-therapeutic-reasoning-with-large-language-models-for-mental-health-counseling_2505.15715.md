## Abstract
**[Key point]**: Existing LLM-based mental health support systems lack clinical grounding, specifically in explicit diagnostic reasoning aligned with DSM/ICD standards and the integration of diverse therapeutic modalities beyond basic empathy.
**Main contribution**: The paper proposes PsyLLM, the first LLM designed to systematically integrate both diagnostic and therapeutic reasoning for mental health counseling.
**Key results:**
- A novel automated data synthesis pipeline was developed to process real-world mental health posts from Reddit.
- The pipeline generates multi-turn dialogue structures guided by international diagnostic standards and multiple therapeutic frameworks (CBT, ACT, psychodynamic).
- Rigorous multi-dimensional filtering ensures high-quality, clinically aligned dialogue data.
- A new benchmark and evaluation protocol were introduced to assess counseling quality across four key dimensions.
- PsyLLM significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baseline models on the proposed benchmark.
**Conclusion:**
- Integrating diagnostic and therapeutic reasoning addresses critical limitations in current LLM-based mental health support.
- The model weights and dataset (OpenR1-Psy) have been publicly released to facilitate further research.

## 1 Introduction
**Context:**
- Large language models (LLMs) show significant potential for mental health support due to their ability to generate empathetic responses.
- LLMs can simulate therapeutic conversations, offering scalable mental health assistance.
- However, existing approaches often lack the clinical grounding necessary for real-world psychological counseling.
- Current models frequently fail to provide explicit diagnostic reasoning aligned with clinical standards like DSM or ICD.
- Most existing systems rely on basic empathy or single therapeutic strategies rather than comprehensive clinical reasoning.
- Real-world counseling requires a systematic integration of diagnosis and varied therapeutic interventions.

**Goal:**
- To address the lack of clinical grounding in current LLM-based mental health support systems.
- To develop a model that systematically integrates both diagnostic and therapeutic reasoning.
- To create a high-quality dataset that reflects real-world clinical interactions and reasoning processes.
- To establish a robust evaluation protocol for assessing the quality of mental health counseling generated by LLMs.

**Contributions:**
- Proposal of PsyLLM, a novel LLM architecture for mental health counseling.
- Development of the OpenR1-Psy dataset using an automated data synthesis pipeline.
- Implementation of a pipeline that leverages DSM/ICD standards and multiple therapeutic frameworks.
- Introduction of a new benchmark and evaluation protocol for mental health counseling quality.
- Demonstration that PsyLLM outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in clinical reasoning and counseling quality.
- Public release of model weights and dataset to support community research.
